About This Service

What Is Swedish Relaxation Massage?

Swedish massage is the foundational massage therapy technique used by Registered Massage Therapists worldwide. It uses a combination of long, flowing strokes (effleurage), gentle kneading (petrissage), rhythmic tapping (tapotement), and friction to work the superficial muscle layers, improve blood circulation, and activate the body's natural relaxation response.

Unlike deep tissue massage, which targets deeper muscle layers and chronic structural tension, Swedish massage focuses on the whole body — calming the nervous system, improving lymphatic flow, and releasing the kind of surface-level tension that builds up from everyday stress, screen time, and sedentary work.

It is the most commonly recommended starting point for first-time massage clients and a reliable maintenance treatment for anyone managing ongoing stress or fatigue.

Who Benefits Most

Swedish massage is an excellent choice if you are:

  • New to massage therapy
  • Managing workplace or lifestyle stress
  • Experiencing general muscle tightness (not chronic)
  • Having difficulty sleeping due to tension
  • Looking for a calming, restorative session
  • Recovering from mild physical or mental fatigue
  • Wanting to use your extended health benefits

What to Expect in Your Session

Your first session starts with a brief intake discussion. Ricky Arora RMT will ask about your primary areas of discomfort, pressure preferences (light, medium, or firm), and any health considerations that might affect the treatment.

You'll remain draped with a sheet throughout. Only the area being worked on is uncovered at any point. Pressure is adjusted on request — your comfort and feedback guide the entire treatment.

Sessions typically cover the back, neck, shoulders, and legs, though you can direct the focus to specific areas. Each session closes with a few minutes for you to reorient before getting up.
