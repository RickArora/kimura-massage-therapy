About This Service

What Is Deep Tissue Massage?

Deep tissue massage is a therapeutic massage technique that uses slow, firm, sustained pressure to reach the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. Where Swedish massage works primarily on the superficial muscles for relaxation, deep tissue targets the underlying muscle fibres, fascia, and tendons where chronic tension, adhesions, and trigger points accumulate over time.

The technique involves working across the grain of the muscle (cross-fibre friction), sustained direct pressure on trigger points, and slow stripping strokes along the length of the muscle. This breaks down adhesions — bands of rigid tissue that cause pain, restrict range of motion, and limit blood flow.

Deep tissue massage is not about applying maximum force. Ricky Arora RMT works slowly and methodically, checking in throughout the session to ensure pressure is productive and within your tolerance. Effective deep tissue work is controlled, not aggressive.

Conditions It Addresses

  • Chronic neck and shoulder tension
  • Upper and lower back pain
  • Postural imbalances from desk work
  • Muscle knots and trigger points
  • Tension headaches originating in the neck
  • Repetitive strain and overuse injuries
  • Hip tightness and sciatica-related tension
  • Recovery from soft-tissue injuries

What to Expect — Pressure and Communication

Deep tissue massage is collaborative. Before your session, Ricky will ask about your specific areas of tension, the history of any recurring pain, and your pressure preferences. This shapes the entire treatment.

During the session, you'll experience sustained pressure on tight areas. Some discomfort is normal and expected — often described as a "productive pain" that releases as the tissue responds. If pressure becomes sharp or uncomfortable, communicate immediately. Ricky adjusts in real time.

After a deep tissue session, mild soreness in the treated areas for 24–48 hours is normal and a sign that the tissue is responding. Drinking water and light movement aid recovery.
